Dithered 2025

I come from a third cultural background based between California and Aotearoa, New Zealand—two coasts, immersed in extended global cultural field studies and lived experiences, that have taught me the value of embracing multiple worldviews with empathy. This is one grounding that informs a part of my work in human-environment interaction, technology, and systems, examining our influences on our relationship with environmental spaces.

Beyond

I believe that understanding our environment comes in many combined shapes, whether social, technological, or natural. Understanding this requires transdisciplinary dialogue, curiosity, a low ego, and high integrity. Our purpose should serve human flourishing, not diminish our profound connections to humanity. Intellectual rigor benefits from nuance, evidence, and good faith. This is what I strive to practice throughout, and I believe I have room for growth.

Leadership, whether intellectual or institutional, means listening as much as speaking. Seeing beyond facades, cutting through noise, and finding common threads. It means balancing competing goods, acknowledging complexity, and maintaining both principle and pragmatism.

I'm drawn to people who move comfortably between serious and playful, who genuinely have positive intentions and care for others. I value quality conversations and good banter with thoughtful people across disciplines. Shared adventures, thoughtful exchanges, and serendipitous encounters have been some of my favorite ways to connect.
